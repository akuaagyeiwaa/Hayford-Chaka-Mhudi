About me

The researcher, Akua Agyeiwaa Manieson is a first year PhD candidate at the University of Nebraka, Lincoln, USA. Akua has several years teaching and reseaching at higher levels of education in Ghana. This project on researching and archiving African digital sources is of great interest to Akua, and she hopes that through such projects, she would be able to help archive various African liteary texts

The picture Element

the first was taken at Bridge port Beach, Connecticut, USA, in 2019. This shows the length of my life’s journeys, through Teacher Training College and working as a teacher before embarking on the university as my classmates had done. Between my first and second degrees was a gap of two years that I used teaching at both Kumasi Wesley Girls’ Senior High school and Ghanatta Senior High school between 2004 and 2006. Then after my second degree and settling into marriage, I spent a decade teaching at Central University, Accra, Ghana. In 2018, I began a PhD at the University of Ghana, only to truncate it afterwards, and now in 2022, I found myself at the University of Nebraska, Lincoln, USA. This really is symbolic of my life’s journey, indeed a journey of a thousand miles always begins with a step. I am still on the road of life, and I pray every day that I will have a safe landing, so help me God! .
